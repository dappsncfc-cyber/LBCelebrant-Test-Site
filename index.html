Answering Your Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Currently, in England and Wales, an independent celebrant cannot perform the legal marriage registration. Couples typically complete their legal registration (the "administrative script") at a local register office before or after their personalised, bespoke celebrant ceremony. However, your celebrant-led ceremony is where you share your real vows, exchange rings, and perform custom symbolic rituals (like handfasting) in front of all your family and friends.
To ensure smooth choreography, coordinate cue timings with venue suppliers, and allow standard time for stationery or Order of Service layout printing, all scripts must be approved and locked in your Secure Client Portal at least 14 days before the ceremony date.
As independent celebrants, we can incorporate a wide range of symbolic rituals, including traditional handfasting (binding of wrists with silk cords), ring-warming (where guests pass your rings to infuse them with good wishes), memory wish-trees, candle-lighting, or sand-blending rituals. All of these can be customised to match your family values.

Privacy Policy

UK GDPR & Data Protection Act 2018 Compliance Statement

Last Updated: July 2026

Welcome to the Privacy Policy of Lisa B Celebrant. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data in strict compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Data Controller

Lisa B Celebrant is the Data Controller and is responsible for your personal data. If you have any questions regarding this policy or wish to exercise any of your data protection rights, please contact us at: privacy@lb-celebrant.co.uk.

2. The Personal Data We Collect

We collect, store, and process personal data necessary to provide bespoke celebrancy services, including:

  • Identity Data: Full names, preferred pronouns, and marital status.
  • Contact Data: Email addresses, telephone numbers, and billing addresses.
  • Ceremony Data: Ceremony type, planned date, geographical venue, custom vow layouts, guest counts, and answers provided in your story questionnaires (such as details of your relationship, memories of deceased loved ones, or family histories).
  • Technical Data: Your IP address, browser type, cookies, and login session tokens within our client portal.
  • Planning Vault Data: Any ceremony schedules, reading PDFs, music cue lists, or photographs you upload to your private vault.

3. Lawful Bases for Data Processing

Under the UK GDPR, we rely on the following lawful bases to process your personal data:

  • Contractual Performance: Processing is necessary to deliver the bespoke writing and officiating services you have booked.
  • Consent: By filling out the Story Questionnaire and uploading items to the Document Vault, you provide explicit consent for us to process sensitive personal details (such as religious, philosophical, or deeply personal family stories) specifically to draft your custom script.
  • Legitimate Interests: To manage client accounts safely, run technical diagnostic troubleshooting, and protect our servers from unauthorized access or malicious traffic.

4. Data Storage and Cloud Security

All client questionnaires, draft scripts, comments, and uploaded files are stored securely in Google Firebase databases utilizing encrypted EU regional data centers. We enforce strict Firebase security and row-level access rules, ensuring your data remains fully isolated and protected from third-party interception.

5. Data Retention

We only retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes we collected it for, including the execution of your ceremony, the billing of remaining balances, and satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting obligations. Typically, active planning workspaces and uploaded vault materials are safely archived and locked 30 days after your ceremony date, and completely deleted within 3 years, unless you request earlier removal.

6. Your Data Protection Rights

Under UK law, you have rights we are committed to upholding, including:

  • The Right of Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • The Right to Rectification: Request corrections to any incomplete or inaccurate data.
  • The Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request that we delete your personal data from our databases and storage vaults.
  • The Right to Object or Restrict Processing: Challenge how your data is being handled.
  • The Right to Data Portability: Request the transfer of your data to another organization.

7. How to Complain

If you have any concerns about our use of your personal data, you can make a formal complaint to us directly at privacy@lb-celebrant.co.uk.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK's independent data protection authority, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), if you remain dissatisfied: www.ico.org.uk.
